Guidelines for CH4 Purification to Bio-Methane Specs

1. Target (Bio-Methane Specification)

Must upgrade quality to closely resemble Natural Gas (NG)

  • CH4: > 95% (Some standards > 97%)
  • CO2: < 2-4%
  • H2S: < 5 mg/m³ (or ppm)
  • Moisture: < 32 mg/m³

2. Pre-treatment

Reduce H2S and moisture before the main upgrading process

  • Biological Desulfurization: Bio-scrubbers utilizing sulfur-oxidizing bacteria
  • Activated Carbon: Adsorbs residual H2S
  • Chilling / Dehumidification: Lowers temperature to condense and remove water

3. Upgrading Technologies

Separate CO2 to increase CH4 concentration

  • Water Scrubbing: Uses high-pressure water to dissolve CO2 (popular due to simplicity)
  • Pressure Swing Adsorption (PSA): Uses adsorbent materials to capture CO2 under pressure
  • Membrane Separation: Uses semi-permeable membranes to separate gas molecules (compact footprint)
  • Amine Wash: Uses chemical solvents to absorb CO2 (highest purity efficiency)
